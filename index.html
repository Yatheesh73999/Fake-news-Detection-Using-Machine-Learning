HOW Verita WORKS
STEP 01
Fetch News
Pulls real-time articles related to your claim using reliable News APIs.
STEP 02
Source Eval
Analyzes the credibility and historical accuracy of the publishing sources.
STEP 03
Stance Detect
Uses NLP semantic similarity to extract evidence and determine if it Supports or Refutes the claim.
STEP 04
Evidence Score
Weighs each piece of evidence based on source credibility and direct matches.
STEP 05
Final Verdict
Aggregates all scores to give a final verdict and a confidence percentage.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
Common FAQ
COMMON
How does the fact-checker work?
▼
Our system uses a 5-step NLP pipeline to retrieve related news, evaluate source credibility, detect the stance of evidence, score the evidence strength, and calculate a final aggregate verdict based on all factors.
COMMON
What does "Likely True", "Unverified", or "Likely False" mean?
▼
These are the verdicts our system gives after analyzing your claim. Each verdict comes with a confidence score so you know how certain the result is.
COMMON
Where does the information come from?
▼
We pull data from multiple trusted sources — including Google Fact Check, NewsAPI, The Guardian, and Wikipedia — to give you a well-rounded analysis.
COMMON
What if no fact-check is found?
▼
If no existing fact-check exists for your claim, the system falls back to analyzing news articles directly and scores them based on relevance, credibility of the source, and how closely they relate to your claim.
Technical FAQ
TECH
How does the verification process actually work behind the scenes?
▼
It uses an NLP pipeline: retrieving articles via API, evaluating source credibility, detecting the stance of evidence using embeddings, scoring the evidence strength, and mathematically calculating an aggregate verdict.
TECH
How does the Model understand the meaning of my claim?
▼
It uses Sentence Transformers to convert your claim and the news content into dense vector representations. It then calculates cosine similarity to find the most semantically relevant sentences.
TECH
How is the final verdict actually calculated?
▼
The verdict engine aggregates individual scores of retrieved evidence. "SUPPORTS" evidence adds to the true probability, while "REFUTES" adds to the false probability. The final verdict is based on the highest probability.
TECH
What sources does the system pull from, and why these specifically?
▼
It pulls from trusted journalism through NewsAPI. Sources like Reuters, AP, and BBC are highly weighted because of their strict editorial guidelines and reliable factual reporting.
TECH
How does the system decide if a news article is actually relevant to my claim?
▼
It measures semantic similarity between your claim and article sentences. If the similarity score exceeds a defined threshold, the article is considered relevant evidence.
TECH
What happens when sources disagree with each other?
▼
The system weighs conflicting evidence based on source credibility, similarity, and keyword matches. A strong, credible supporting article will outweigh a weak, unverified refuting article.
TECH
Why might two similar claims get different confidence scores?
▼
Confidence scores depend heavily on the quality and quantity of retrieved evidence. If one claim has direct factual matches from highly credible sources, it will score higher than a claim with only weak semantic similarities.
TECH
Does a high confidence score guarantee the claim is true or false?
▼
Not strictly. A high confidence score means the model found strong, highly relevant, and credible evidence that aligns with its verdict. However, it's always recommended to read the provided evidence directly for nuance.
